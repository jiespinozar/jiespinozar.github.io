Research

I'm currently a PhD candidate at the Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile working on exoplanets under the supervision of Dr Cristobal Petrovich, and in close collaboration with Dr Rafael Brahm and Dr Guðmundur Stefánsson. My PhD thesis, titled Constraining the flatness of planetary systems, studies the architectures of planetary systems to understand their formation and evolution histories. In order to do that, I follow both observational and theoretical approaches.

On the observational side, my research focuses on measuring stellar obliquities using the Rossiter–McLaughlin effect seen in the radial velocities during a planetary transit. I recently measured the stellar obliquity of the two hot Neptunes HATS-38 b and WASP- 139 b, finding that they both have nearly polar orbits.
